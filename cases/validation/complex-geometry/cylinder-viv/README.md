# VIV of a Cylinder (Khalak & Williamson)

## Physics

Vortex-induced vibration (VIV) of an elastically mounted circular cylinder at low mass-damping (m*=10.1, zeta=0.0011). The cylinder oscillates transversely due to lock-in with vortex shedding.

## Regimes (Khalak & Williamson 1997)

| Region | Ur | A/D | Branch |
|--------|-----|-----|--------|
| Initial | 3-5 | <0.3 | --- |
| Upper | 5-9 | 0.3-0.92 | 2S vortex mode |
| Lower | 9-11 | 0.35-0.45 | 2P vortex mode |
| Desynchronization | >11 | <0.3 | --- |

## Key Phenomena

- Lock-in: vortex shedding frequency locks to structural natural frequency
- Upper branch: large amplitude (A/D up to 0.92), 2S vortex pattern
- Lower branch: moderate amplitude, 2P vortex pattern
- Hysteresis between branches
